About Our Lab

Bridging Clinical Care and Research

Dr. Gabriel Ghiaur is an Associate Professor of Oncology and a member of the Johns Hopkins Kimmel Cancer Center. As a physician-scientist, his primary interest lies in normal and malignant hematopoiesis, specifically investigating how the microenvironment contributes to cell extrinsic drug resistance and the persistence of minimal residual disease.

His dual role as a laboratory-based investigator and clinician allows for unique translational potential in stem cell therapeutics, acute leukemia, and multiple myeloma. The lab combines advanced research with clinical insights to bridge the gap between molecular mechanisms and patient care.

Key Research Areas

We focus on three interconnected pillars of cancer biology.

Drug Resistance & Microenvironment

Investigating the role of the bone marrow microenvironment in cell extrinsic drug resistance and the persistence of minimal residual disease.

Hematopoiesis & Stem Cells

Studying normal and malignant hematopoiesis to understand the fundamental biology of leukemia stem cells and their interaction with the niche.

Translational Therapeutics

Developing novel therapeutic strategies for acute leukemia and multiple myeloma, with a focus on overcoming microenvironment-mediated protection.

Clonal Hematopoiesis (CHIP)

Investigating Clonal Hematopoiesis of Indeterminate Potential (CHIP) to understand its role in aging, inflammation, and leukemogenesis.
